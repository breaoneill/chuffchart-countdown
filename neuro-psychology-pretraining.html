Dementia & Communication Study Plan

This plan is designed to fit around full-time work, with a lighter schedule during your post-surgery recovery (starting 15 October 2025). It mirrors the focus areas of a psychology degree, with emphasis on dementia, non-verbal communication, and research skills.

Phase 1 – Foundations (Aug–Sep 2025)

  • Goal: Learn the basics of psychology and the brain.
  • Weekly time: 3–4 hours over 2–3 evenings.
  • Courses:
    • Introduction to Psychology – University of Toronto (Coursera)
    • The Brain and Behaviour – Monash University (FutureLearn)

Phase 2 – Recovery Window (Oct 15–31 2025)

  • Goal: Gentle learning during recovery, focus on dementia basics.
  • Daily time: 30–45 mins.
  • Courses:
    • Understanding Dementia – University of Tasmania

Phase 3 – Deepening & Application (Nov–Dec 2025)

  • Goal: Learn dementia subtypes and non-verbal communication skills.
  • Weekly time: 3–4 hours over 2–3 evenings.
  • Courses:
    • The Many Faces of Dementia – UCL (FutureLearn)
    • Non-Verbal Communication – UC Davis (Coursera)

Phase 4 – Research & Reflection (Jan–Feb 2026)

  • Goal: Gain basic research literacy and decide on formal degree plans.
  • Weekly time: 2–3 hours.
  • Courses:
    • Introduction to Statistics – Stanford University (YouTube)
